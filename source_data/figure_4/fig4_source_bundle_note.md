Figure 4 source bundle note

This source bundle records the input data prepared for Figure 4 before plotting. It separates the ten PH908 adversarial sensitivity states, the matched R1a-comparator envelope thresholds, the positive-control recovery trace, the source-visibility burden trace, the primary-layer visibility-bias reversal summary, the equal-n rarefaction adequacy trace, and the synthetic visibility-degradation controls. Versioned source fields that contain `sensitivity` refer to adversarial sensitivity states retained for computational traceability; within the analysis they are interpreted as mask-choice sensitivity states, not source claims.

Weak directional compatibility means source adequacy above one and negative diversity contrast. Weak directional compatibility is not treated as load-bearing evidence by itself. Matched-envelope entry additionally requires PH908 to enter the state-matched R1a-comparator envelope defined by the R-Z280 and R-M458 comparator values under the same branch-resolution, ambiguity-handling and source-mask state.

The source-visibility burden trace reports, for each adversarial state, the source-adequacy multiplier still required for PH908 to reach the matched R1a-comparator source-adequacy threshold. This is a public-tree visibility-burden sensitivity calculation, not a population-size estimate, bias correction, or origin inference.

The primary-layer visibility-bias reversal summary reports the headline source-adequacy and source-diversity multipliers required to move the primary PH908 YFull coordinate to basic compatibility or to the less stringent primary matched R1a-comparator threshold. These values are sensitivity bounds, not estimates of unsampled individuals.

The equal-n rarefaction trace is generated by `scripts/07_generate_figure4_rarefaction_and_degradation.py` from audited scoreable-cell and positive-cell counts in `source_data/core_figure4_rarefaction_degradation/figure4_equal_n_rarefaction_metric_source.csv`. It supports the adequacy interpretation of low-common-n failures and should not be read as a universal all-grid validation claim.

The synthetic visibility-degradation trace is generated by the same numbered script from the matched R1a comparator rows in `tables/11_branch_unit_reliability_pass_matrix.csv`, the benchmark envelope values in `tables/26_benchmark_audit_card_matrix.csv`, and the retained-visibility and rule inputs in `source_data/core_figure4_rarefaction_degradation`. It applies deterministic source-terminal and source-country thinning together with audited branch-layer collapse, then recomputes source adequacy, carries the corresponding diversity contrast, applies country-visibility gates, and reports the resulting compatibility state. This is an operating-characteristic test of the framework under biased observation, not a population-size estimate.

The Figure 4 source rows are anchored to generated Table 6, the core YFull highlighted-contract source, the branch-unit reliability matrix, the benchmark audit-card matrix, and the manuscript adequacy-sensitivity summary. Individual R-Z280 and R-M458 comparator values are carried here only as cleaned threshold and positive-control fields needed to reproduce the Figure 4 envelope, visibility-burden, rarefaction, degradation, and calibration panels. The bundle uses descriptive branch-resolution, ambiguity-handling, source-mask, and boundary-context labels throughout.

The ten tested PH908 adversarial sensitivity states all satisfy weak directional compatibility, but none enters the state-matched R1a-comparator envelope. Six states are positive-control-passing and four are retained for transparency but are not load-bearing because positive controls fail. The full equal-n rarefaction grid is not uniformly positive, but failures concentrate at low common-n and disappear at common-n >= 50. In the synthetic degradation controls, the R1a positive controls retain 10/12 basic-compatible branch-layer states at retained source visibility of 100 percent and 50 percent, but 0/12 at 25 percent after the source-country visibility gate is applied.

These files should not be interpreted as evidence of PH908 geographic origin, ethnic identity, population continuity, migration route, or population-level probability.
