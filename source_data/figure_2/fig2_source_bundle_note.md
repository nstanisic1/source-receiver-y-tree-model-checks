Figure 2 source bundle note

This source bundle records the input data prepared for Figure 2 before plotting. It separates the broad YFull reliability-atlas background from the predeclared focused contracts highlighted in the figure.

The broad atlas layer is derived one-to-one from Table 14 and should be read as a descriptive reliability field. It is not a global-origin discovery screen. The highlighted overlay is derived from Table 13 and records seven predeclared focused contracts, not screen-discovered atlas hits. The decision-gate inset is derived from Table 3 and summarizes promotion, support-or-boundary demotion, and no-call/deferred states as figure-reading grammar rather than as additional empirical results.

A_s denotes source adequacy. D denotes ln(q2_receiver/q2_source). The `source_receiver` fields in Figure 2 source data refer to the specified downstream source–receiver geometry tested by the audit; they are not historical-event labels. The deepest-branch source–receiver call records the directional deepest-layer criterion before reliability-state classification and is not, by itself, a reported claim.

The source–receiver reliability score is a display and tiering coordinate. Lower values indicate stronger display-level reliability under the atlas scoring convention. The source–receiver reliability score is not a population-level probability, effect size, demographic-disruption measure, or origin score.

Figure 2 source files should not be interpreted as evidence of exact geographic origin, ethnic identity, population continuity, migration route, or population-level probability. The decision-gate inset should not be read as reclassifying any atlas row; it explains the predefined criteria by which candidate results are promoted, demoted to support or boundary status, or left as no-call/deferred.
