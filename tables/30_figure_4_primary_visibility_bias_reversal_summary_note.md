Table 30 summarizes the primary-layer visibility-bias reversal values used in Figure 4.

The rows quantify how much hidden source-side public-tree visibility would be required to move the primary PH908 YFull coordinate to basic compatibility or to the less stringent primary matched R1a-comparator threshold. For source adequacy, the multiplier is the target `A_s` divided by the observed PH908 `A_s`. For source diversity, the multiplier is `exp(D_observed - D_target)`, holding receiver diversity fixed.

These values are sensitivity bounds, not estimates of unsampled individuals, not a correction for public-tree bias, and not evidence of PH908 geographic origin, ethnic identity, population continuity, migration route, or population-level probability.
