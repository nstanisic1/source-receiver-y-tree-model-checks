The deepest-branch source-receiver call records whether the deepest-layer source-receiver criterion is directionally compatible before reliability-state classification; it is not, by itself, a manuscript claim. The atlas reliability state incorporates branch-unit sensitivity, country perturbation, scoreability, and scale-sensitivity checks.

Using only deepest-layer directional geometry, 1,163 of 2,466 strict ordered contracts are compatible. After reliability classification, 118 remain robust source-receiver compatible; the other directionally compatible rows are demoted to fragile or scale-sensitive, no-call or underpowered, or broad-root-deferred states.

Lower source-receiver reliability-score values indicate stronger display-level reliability under the atlas scoring convention. The source-receiver reliability score is not a population-level probability, effect size, demographic-disruption measure, or origin score.
